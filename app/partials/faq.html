When Should I Replace My Existing System?

If your furnace or heat pump is nearing the end of its lifespan, you might want to consider replacement sooner rather than later. It’s easier and cheaper to replace a furnace during the warmer months than waiting until it breaks down in the middle of January. Our experienced heating contractors can install a new heating system with minimal disruption and without making a mess. Here are some indications that it might be time for heater replacement:

  • Furnace or heat pump needs frequent repairs
  • Energy bills are increasing every month
  • House is dry and dusty in winter
  • Indoor air temperature does not match the thermostat setting
  • Some rooms are too cold; other rooms are too hot
  • Furnace is over 15 years old
  • Heat pump is over 10 years old
  • Furnace or heat pump has not been properly maintained

When Should I Repair My Existing System?

Heat pump repair if you notice any of the following symptoms:

  • Heat pump or furnace is making strange noises
  • Furnace burner flame is yellow instead of blue
  • You or your family have flu-like symptoms

Strange noises in any kind of heating system are almost always a cause for concern, whereas a yellow furnace flame or unexplained flu-like symptoms can signal a carbon monoxide leak.

Why Should I Perform Preventative Maintenance?

Whether your heater is new or getting on in years, it should be inspected and maintained annually, preferably before the cold weather kicks in. Annual service will save money on repair bills, minimize breakdowns and ensure that your heating system is operating safely. A well-maintained heating system will function more efficiently, consume less energy, cost less to operate and have a longer lifespan.

Should I Upgrade My Existing System for A Energy Saving Installation?

Replacing your existing furnace or heat pump can save you money over time. A new heater with a programmable thermostat, or a zoned system that allows you to heat only the rooms you use can significantly reduce monthly energy costs. Together with the savings you’ll see every month with a highly efficient furnace or heat pump, a new heater can eventually pay for itself. The heat pumps and furnaces of today are highly evolved systems that generate more heat while consuming less energy. It’s not unusual to see substantial reductions in power bills immediately after an energy-efficient heating replacement.

Should I Be Concerned About Air Quality?

According to the EPA, as much as 60% of the air that enters your home comes from your basement or crawl space. If your basement or crawl space has water or moisture issues, chances are you have indoor air quality problems too. Mold and other spores grow readily in a high moisture environment and those spores then travel to your living areas as air naturally rises from your basement or crawl space to the upper levels of your home. By waterproofing your basement or crawl space, you can alleviate the issue of moisture and wetness below. If you want to take that one step further, you can install an indoor air quality system to assure that the air that you are breathing in your home is pure, clean and healthier than it is now.
